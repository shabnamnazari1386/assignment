Kabul
Kabul is the capital and largest city of Afghanistan, located in the eastern part of the country. It serves as the country's political, cultural, and economic center. Kabul has a long history, dating back over 3,500 years, and is strategically situated in a narrow valley between the Hindu Kush mountains.

Herat
Herat is a city and province in western Afghanistan, serving as the regional capital. It's known for its strategic, commercial, and cultural significance, with a rich history and many surviving Islamic monuments. Herat is the third-largest city in Afghanistan and is located near the Iranian border.

Bamyan
Bamyan also referred to as the “Shining Light” and “Valley of Gods” is one of the oldest cities in central and South Asia. Inhabited since the third century BC, Bamyan is located in the central highlands, known as the peaceful Hazarajat or Hazaristan, Afghanistan.

Balkh Bastan
Balkh Province, in northern Afghanistan, is known for its historical significance and diverse population. Its capital, Mazar-i-Sharif, is a major urban center and the fourth largest city in Afghanistan. The province has a population of approximately 1.5 million, including various ethnic groups like Tajiks, Hazaras, and Uzbeks. Balkh shares borders with Uzbekistan, Turkmenistan, and Tajikistan.
